UNESCO (CGW4U)

United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization

Building peace in the minds of men & women

Quizlet: Make sure to visit the Quizlet here.

History & Description

  • Established on November 4th, 1946 in London, UK.
  • The aftermath of the Second World War.
  • Part of the United Nations.
    • Guarantee access to high quality education.
    • Reinforce cultural acceptance between nations.
    • Preserve historical and natural monuments.
    • Advance technology to assist in the distribution of resources and energy.
    • Protect the safety of basic human rights.
    • Promotes scientific research and democratic organizations in many developing countries.
  • Global priorities are currently: Africa and Gender Equality.
  • Currently based in Paris, France.

Management

  • Umbrella organization that has many subdivisions and relations to other UN organizations.
  • “Formal Associates” include:
    • International Baccalaureate
    • International Association of Universities
    • International Federation of Journalists
  • Director General - Audrey Azoulay
  • Deputy Director General - Xing Qu

Liaison in Canada

  • With UNESCO being an intercontinental organization, it operates many regional offices.
  • The closest liaison regional office is the UN headquarters in New York.
  • Since Canada was a co-founder, UNESCO has a national liaison to Ottawa.
    • Under the title of the Canadian Commission for UNESCO.
  • The CCUNESCO works with the Canadian government to protect fundamental values, such as human rights and freedoms, and makes Canada’s influence felt on the international stage.
  • A member from Canada, Napoléon LeBlanc chaired the 1978 General Conference.

Canadian WHS

  • Some famous World Heritage Sites in Canada include cultural landmarks such as:
    • L’Anse aux Meadows, Norse viking settlement in Newfoundland predating the arrival of Christopher Columbus.
    • Old District of Québec, which retains the same building style as Old France.
    • Rideau Canal, which was built to protect the Colony of Canada from the United States in the early 1800’s.
  • There are also natural landmarks where ancient fossils have been discovered, such as:
    • Canadian Rocky Mountains
    • Dinosaur Provincial Park
    • Mistaken Point

Member Nations

  • UNESCO is a global organization which is partnered with 193 member countries.
  • Many countries are a part of UNESCO, including:
    • The UK
    • Russia
    • Brazil
    • Nigeria
    • China
  • Two notable exceptions:
    • United States
    • Israel

Global WHS

  • More than a thousand world heritage sites. 1121 as of January 2020:
    • 869 Cultural
    • 213 Natural
    • 39 Mixed
  • The most notable cultural sites that you may have heard of include:
    • The Great Wall of China
    • The Taj Mahal
    • The Leaning Tower of Pisa
    • The Statue of Liberty
    • The Eiffel Tower
  • Some natural sites consist of:
    • The Great Barrier Reef
    • Yosemite National Park
    • Easter Island

Criticisms/Controversies

  • Although World Heritage sites are claimed to be a positive thing, evidence for the inverse exists.
  • The Guardian states that UNESCO is doing more harm than good by labeling:
    • Tourists come to the sites and change the people’s culture and landscape.
    • Degrades the site even more.
    • Jetty Markets in Malaysia - locals turned from fishing and hunting to full tourist attractions.
  • Vineyards in Italy - same problem as the Jetty Markets (Forbes).
    • Locals pressured to plant more - soil began to slowly erode, destroying the habitat and ecosystem.
  • Withdrawal of the United States and Israel from UNESCO in 2019.
    • Due to an alleged anti-Israel bias.
    • Relating to religious and political conflict.

Improvements & Conclusion

  • Can improve by managing their funds a bit more efficiently:
    • By partnering with country governments a bit more.
    • Establishing more regional liaison offices.
  • Designating more world heritage sites in places like Australia.

  • All in all, despite some backlash and controversial decisions, UNESCO does much to preserve special areas in the world for future generations.
  • Does much to help educate and maintain freedoms in developing countries in Africa and Asia.
  • Keeps history alive in developed countries such as Canada.
  • One of the most important United Nations’ agencies.
